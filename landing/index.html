Features

Built around what a day off actually costs

Small tools that share one calendar — mark a day once, and the statistics, quotas, and salary math all update together.

Month calendar view

Gregorian and lunar dates, statutory holidays, makeup workdays, and weekends — every day's status readable at a glance.

Leave statistics

Monthly heatmaps and yearly charts of leave by type, so you always know how many days you've used — and on what.

Real working-day math

Weekends and public holidays are excluded, makeup workdays included — attendance reflects the calendar you actually work.

Salary deductions

Set your base salary, insurance and housing fund, and per-type deduction rates. RestCal estimates take-home pay for any month.

Ticket reminders

Train tickets in China open 15 days ahead. RestCal flags the on-sale date for each statutory holiday, so you book before the rush.

CSV import & export

One click exports every record; imports are validated before anything is overwritten. Backups and migrations stay boring.

Leave note templates

Pick a leave type, dates, and reason — get a formal, ready-to-send leave note for personal, sick, or annual leave.

Yours, locally

Everything runs in your browser and stays in local storage. Works offline as a PWA, or as a portable Windows app. No account, ever.

Who it's for

For people who count their days

RestCal is deliberately small. If any of these sound like you, it will probably fit.

记录

The record keeper

Office workers who want every sick day and half-afternoon in one place, with monthly and yearly totals ready when review season comes.

算账

The paycheck realist

Anyone deciding whether a day off is worth it. See the deduction before you send the request — not on payday.

规划

The holiday strategist

Bridge two annual-leave days into a nine-day break. Plan around makeup workdays instead of being surprised by them.

极简

The tool minimalist

One HTML page. No login, no sync, no subscription. Open it, mark a day, close it — your attendance record, nothing else.

Open source

One page of HTML, MIT licensed

RestCal is a personal project built with plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — no framework, no build step, no analytics. Clone it and open index.html, and it works. Read the whole source in an afternoon, or fork it to match your company's leave rules.

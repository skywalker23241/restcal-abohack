Features

Built around what a day off actually costs

Small tools that share one calendar — mark a day once, and the statistics, quotas, and salary math all update together.

Day, week, month & year views

Switch from a tear-off day page to weekly, monthly, or yearly views while keeping lunar dates, holidays and makeup workdays visible.

Monthly & yearly statistics

Switch between monthly and yearly analysis, with annual and comp-leave allowance usage always visible alongside the charts.

Real working-day math

Weekends and public holidays are excluded, makeup workdays included — attendance reflects the calendar you actually work.

Pay calculation & payslips

Set salary and deduction rules once, then estimate monthly take-home pay and download a clean payslip image.

Ticket reminders

A focused tool card shows the next statutory-holiday ticket sale date and expands into the full yearly reminder list.

CSV import & export

CSV and optional WebDAV backup live together in Settings, with validated imports and merge-safe restoration.

Leave note templates

Pick dates and a reason, preview the result, and download a clean leave-note image ready to send.

Yours, locally

Everything runs locally by default, works offline as a PWA or Windows app, and only uses WebDAV when you choose to configure it.

Who it's for

For people who count their days

RestCal is deliberately small. If any of these sound like you, it will probably fit.

记录

The record keeper

Office workers who want every sick day and half-afternoon in one place, with monthly and yearly totals ready when review season comes.

算账

The paycheck realist

Anyone deciding whether a day off is worth it. See the deduction before you send the request — not on payday.

规划

The holiday strategist

Bridge two annual-leave days into a nine-day break. Plan around makeup workdays instead of being surprised by them.

极简

The tool minimalist

No required account, subscription, or cloud dependency. Keep everything local, or opt into CSV and WebDAV backup when you need it.

Open source

Plain web technology, MIT licensed

RestCal is built with plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — no framework and no analytics. Clone it, run the local server, or package the included Windows app. Read the source in an afternoon, or fork it to match your company's leave rules.
